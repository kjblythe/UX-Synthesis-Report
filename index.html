Three findings · One direction
01

The app landed. All seven wanted to use it daily.

Satisfaction averaged 6.6/7, task ease 6.4/7 — well above the industry benchmark of 5.5. The most critical scorer still said she would replace her current app with this one; said she'd replace her handwritten gratitude journal.

02

AI nutrition is the breakout. Photo logging is the adoption gatekeeper.

Six of seven chose photo logging unprompted — multiple surprised by the AI's recognition accuracy. tied her willingness to track daily to the photo feature: if she can photograph, she logs every meal; if she has to type, she won't.

6.7/7 SEQ on meal logging

Affordances blend into the polished dark surface.

The information architecture is sound. The problem is that interactive elements — navigation, action buttons, confirmations — don't visually announce themselves against the app's dark aesthetic. A visual styling pass, not a structural rebuild.

Bottom navigation fades into the background

Semi-transparent on a dark surface reads as decorative. on first use.

The + button doesn't announce itself

Correct pattern, insufficient visual weight. . Those who did used it correctly the first time.

Toast confirmations read as more content

Styled identically to insight cards. : “I did not know it was done until I knew it was done.”

Wellness rings are treated as primary navigation

Colourful, prominent, intuitive. decoded them unprompted — \u201Cgood range, about three-quarters of where you should be.\u201D

Insight cards carry the home screen

Contextual, action-oriented content was where participants engaged first. The home-card path is the one they found on their own.

Why we're confident the architecture is sound

rated herself lowest on tech comfort (5/7). She uses Apple Health once a week for step counting \u2014 no nutrition apps, no AI tools.
