01 · Executive Summary
n=7 · April 7, 2026

Above benchmark.
Across every task.

In April 2026, BI X conducted in-person moderated usability testing with 7 GLP-1 patients at a professional research facility to evaluate the interactive prototype ahead of the planned 2-month diary study pilot.

Participants completed six structured tasks simulating a full day with the app — from morning exploration through medication logging, meal tracking, AI coach interaction, learning content, and evening reflection.

Three findings · One direction
01

Participants responded overwhelmingly positively to the app experience.

Satisfaction averaged 6.6/7. SEQ task ease averaged 6.4/7 — well above the industry benchmark of 5.5/7 established across 10,000+ users. All 7 participants expressed willingness to use the app daily; two said they'd replace their current health app entirely.

6.6/7 satisfaction · 6.4/7 SEQ · 7/7 willing
02

AI-powered nutrition features drove the strongest engagement.

Photo-based meal logging emerged as a breakout feature — 6/7 participants chose it unprompted, with multiple expressing genuine surprise at the AI's recognition accuracy. Participants consistently requested more depth in nutritional tracking.

6/7 chose photo unprompted
03

The primary usability gap is visual styling — not information architecture.

Interactive elements (bottom navigation, action buttons, completion notifications) blend into the app's polished dark aesthetic. Confirmed by the participant with the lowest self-reported tech comfort (5/7), who navigated every feature intuitively on first attempt.

A CSS fix · not a redesign

How we measured ease

A 1–7 scale. We hit 6.4.

The Single Ease Question is the UX industry's yardstick. After every task, participants answer: “How easy or difficult was this?” on a 7-point scale.

Because it's standardized, our scores can be compared against thousands of other products. The industry benchmark sits at 5.5/7 — the 50th percentile across 10,000+ users.

Our overall mean is 6.4/7. Every task cleared the benchmark.
